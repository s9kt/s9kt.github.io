Current issues with social media
 • Lack of oversight and accountability
 • Facebook, now named Meta, since its founding been involved in a variety of controversies and legal disputes over its privacy practices, most famously the Cambridge Analytica scandal in 2018
 • SnapChat has faced lawsuits over the degree of drug dealing that occurs over the platform due to its “disappearing messages” feature
 • Discord banned more than a million accounts in the first half of 2022 alone, mostly for child safety violations as well as exploitative content
How would a new agency be created? Who would run it?
  • The independent agency would be a bill passed by congress. It would probably require slightly weakening Section 230 by adding an exception for this agency to do its job
 • The agency would be headed by the commission chair, likely an expert in law and technology
 • The agency would be a commission composed of advisors, lawyers, and policymakers from the appropriate senate and house committees
What would the agency do? What are its goals?
  • Consulting with advisors and researchers, the commission would be responsible for the research and development of new ways to make social media safer, like the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration does for vehicles
 • Also like the NHTSA, the commission could set standards for social media platforms to maintain, such as standards for protecting against misinformation, a clear definition and policy for hate speech, and an effort to incite productive conversation rather than inflammatory
 • Require platforms to be transparent in their efforts with the public and report to the commission whenever necessary
